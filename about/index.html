About

"Be guided by beauty. I really mean that. Pretty much everything I've done has had an aesthetic component, at least to me. Now you might think "well, building a company that's trading bonds, what's so aesthetic about that?" But, what's aesthetic about it is doing it right. Geting the right kind of people, and approaching the problem, and doing it right [...] it's a beautiful thing to do something right."

- Jim Simons

Any great creative endeavor, whether it's engineering or artistic, stems from a pursuit of elegance—the quiet clarity that arises when something is done right. Whether I'm trimming the fat off of time constrained functions, simplifying complexity, or debugging a frustrating knot of code, there is a distinct beauty in finally achieving, not just knowledge of a solution, but understanding of the problem.

I'm a software engineer with a strong focus on performance, correctness, and clarity. My background spans systems programming (primarily in C/C++), GPU computation with Vulkan and OpenGL, and secure software design. I work best at the intersection of raw capability and thoughtful restraint—writing code that’s both powerful and understandable.

My guiding principle is to not just have a solution, but also to do it right. That means choosing the right abstractions, asking the hard questions early, and never settling for a hack when a principled solution is well within reach. There's value in doing the hard things well.
